# Financial Feedback Loop

Parent: `docs/architecture/ventureos-architecture.md`

Source Of Truth: This file is the source of truth for Financial Feedback Loop architecture.

## Purpose

Financial Feedback Loop ensures every capital allocation eventually becomes organizational learning.

Capital allocation outcomes must improve future investment decisions, portfolio learning, capital efficiency, Enterprise Value analysis, and governed strategy without allowing learning to silently change policy.

## Architecture Flow

Capital Allocation

-> Execution

-> Business Outcome

-> Expected ROI

-> Actual ROI

-> Variance Analysis

-> Lessons Learned

-> Learning Engine

-> Enterprise Knowledge Graph

-> Future Investment Decisions

## Expected Vs Actual ROI

Expected ROI is the return forecast recorded before or during a governed capital decision.

Actual ROI is the measured return after execution, milestone completion, Venture outcome, investment outcome, acquisition outcome, shutdown, or other lifecycle completion.

Expected and actual ROI must preserve:

- Evidence used.
- Assumptions.
- Time horizon.
- Capital amount.
- Scope.
- Confidence.
- Policy and approval context.
- Outcome measurement date.

## Variance Analysis

Variance Analysis compares expected ROI with actual ROI and explains the gap.

Variance analysis must identify:

- Forecast error.
- Execution variance.
- Market variance.
- Cost variance.
- Timing variance.
- Evidence quality variance.
- Strategic fit variance.
- Founder override impact where applicable.

## Success Attribution

Success attribution identifies why a capital allocation worked.

Attribution may include:

- Strong Thesis alignment.
- High-quality Evidence.
- Correct market timing.
- Effective execution.
- Efficient capital use.
- Strong pricing.
- Strong distribution.
- Operational leverage.
- Portfolio synergy.

## Failure Attribution

Failure attribution identifies why a capital allocation underperformed.

Attribution may include:

- Weak evidence.
- Thesis mismatch.
- Bad timing.
- Cost overrun.
- Market weakness.
- Poor execution.
- Incorrect assumptions.
- Capital inefficiency.
- Governance exception.
- External shock.

## Investment Confidence Calibration

Investment confidence calibration compares pre-decision confidence with actual outcomes.

The goal is to improve future confidence estimates, not to rewrite historical decisions.

## Strategy Improvement

Strategy improvement converts financial outcome patterns into evidence-backed recommendations for Thesis, Hypothesis, Opportunity Score, Strategic Review, Portfolio Governance, and Capital Allocation improvement.

Strategy improvement does not automatically change policy.

## Capital Efficiency Learning

Capital efficiency learning identifies which Ventures, Capabilities, workflows, markets, strategies, and execution patterns create the strongest return per unit of capital.

## Portfolio Learning

Portfolio learning identifies cross-Venture and cross-Portfolio patterns, including concentration risk, correlated risk, follow-on success, capital pacing, and repeatable capital allocation patterns.

Portfolio learning must preserve tenant, Organization, Portfolio, Venture, and Policy Engine boundaries.

## Reusable Knowledge Generation

Reusable knowledge generation packages lessons into evidence-backed learning records and graph relationships that can inform future recommendations.

Reusable knowledge must distinguish:

- Confirmed facts.
- Evidence.
- Decisions.
- Assumptions.
- Forecasts.
- Learning.
- Reasoning.

## Rules

- No completed Venture may exit the lifecycle without producing organizational learning.
- Learning never automatically changes policy.
- Learning is always evidence-backed.
- Learning must link to Evidence, Decision, Audit, capital allocation, execution, and outcome records where meaningful.
- Learning recommendations may inform future investment decisions but do not approve them.
- Policy changes require explicit governance approval.
